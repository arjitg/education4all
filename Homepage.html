It started with a pencil.
To make a point.


Education isn't a privilege, it's a birthright. Although we're headquartered in Dubai, we'd like to think of ourselves as global. We've helped support thousands of children in India receive an education and hope to expand to spread your love and care.



What we do:







×

In February 2010, we were in Arasaravankadu, Thiruvavur, Tamil Nadu India with a partner Raghavan, a Chartered Accountant, who runs a free school with 180 children. Apart from giving free education, the school gives milk with healthy cereals (KANJI) everyday for free. In the course of our discussion, he said that Shikshayatan buys milk partly from the market and from the family who maintains Shikshayatan's cow. A family in the village maintains the cow and gives milk at a subsidised rate to the school.




×

In 2013 and 2014, Education4All, a Venkat's initiative, distributed uniforms to over 7500 under-privileged children in 15 states of India: Tamil Nadu, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Goa, Kerala, Orissa, Delhi, Uttarakand, Punjab, Haryana, West Bengal, Gujarat, and Rajasthan.




×

We also collect books of all sorts: textbooks as well as novels. Kindergarten books are used by children for handwriting. Other school texts and novels are being prescribed to the children to broaden their understanding of the English language and grammatical syntax.







×

With our founder, Venkatraman, dubbed the Pencil-Man of Dubai, it is no surprise that we conduct stationery drives every year to help spark the minds of children in rural India. As of October 2015, we have collected over 300,000 stationery items and have distributed them to over 25,000 under-privileged children in 15 states in India.




×

May 2012 saw Education4All organize a concert to raise stationery for the under-privileged. No sponsors were solicited and entry was strictly not cash-based: instead, we accepted stationery. We worked in conjunction with the Consulate General of India in Dubai, and we hope to organize more such concerts in the future.




×

Infrastructure is everything. Through our capstone Purna Shiksha initiative, we aim to provide schools in rural India with better infrastructure like trash cans, teacher training and water filters. Over 3,000 children have received the benefits of this program so far.

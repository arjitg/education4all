AIM for Seva is a charitable trust working with rural and tribal marginalised children, transforming their lives through quality living, education, and healthcare. Founded by Swami Dayananda Saraswati in 2000, the organisation has positively and directly impacted over 10 million people living across 15 Indian States.

AIM for Seva Student Homes:
The flagship project of AIM for Seva is the Student Home. A unique concept, this facilitates a safe shelter for children from far flung areas, enabling them to attend school regularly without worrying about basic needs of food, shelter and clothing. Over and above that, they are educated at a Government school, given after school academic support to stay in the school system and emotional wellness and strength by the trained caregivers who supervise them in the student homes.

All this is provided totally free of cost to the child. The children, mostly first generation school goers, are thus encouraged to finish schooling, and grow up to be self confident, self-aware and well balanced individuals who can contribute to the society.

A unique programme at Aseema is the transformation of children’s art into marketable products. Artistic expression is encouraged in every child and his/her creativity knows no bounds.

At Aseema, art is not taught. It is the unique expression of every child's inner artist. They work under the guidance and expertise of professional artists who take great pains to bring out the best in their students. They are introduced to various styles of work and use several mediums. They are given a great deal of freedom in their creative expression and their work, as a result, is often quite stunning. Aseema exhibits and sells the children’s art and products made from their art at various exhibitions throughout the year, including the Harmony Art Show.

A professional design team works on the children’s paintings and the best images find themselves on a range of high quality products – stationery, tableware, household and gift items. Prices start at Rs.15 & all proceeds are ploughed back into Aseema’s educational projects. The children experience a great sense of joy and achievement to see their work being displayed, appreciated and bought. Through the sale of products, Aseema hopes to eventually become self-sustaining. We invite you to browse through the catalogue to order with Aseema.

Shikshyatan is a school set up in rural India set up by fellow Chartered Accountant, Raghavan, and his wife, Aruna. They left Mumbai in the early 90s and set up Shikshyatan. The school follows the Tamil Nadu syllabus however the method of teaching is one derived from Glenn Doman’s method of teaching. They also encourage children to take part in various other activities, like the arts and sports. They have a mobile science van for physics and chemistry labs which they even take to nearby schools for demonstration of concepts.

The Special Needs Families (SNF) took its first step as a family when a group of families with special needs individuals resolved to begin a new chapter in the lives of the region’s special needs individuals. The first step in 2003 was motivated by a handful of parents whose aim was to achieve a sense of community for their special needs loved ones – this family now stands at over 146 members strong.

SNF aspires to better prepare its students for mainstream life via personal management, functional academics and society participation for leisure and vocational skill development.
